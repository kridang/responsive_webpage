Da Nang has rapidly become one of Vietnam’s most popular destinations. Visitors are drawn to its beaches, such as My Khe and Non Nuoc, as well as cultural and historic attractions. The city offers a mix of natural beauty and modern amenities, including bustling markets and fresh seafood restaurants.

Tourists can explore nearby beaches, ride the cable cars at Ba Na Hills, or walk along the Han River to enjoy the city’s lively atmosphere — especially in the evening when the bridges and skyline are illuminated.

Located about 20 kilometers from Da Nang, Ba Na Hills is a well-known mountain resort that combines French-style architecture with modern attractions. Originally built by French colonists in 1910, it’s now famous for landmarks like the Iconic Golden Bridge, the French Village, and the Linh Ung Pagoda.

Visitors can enjoy cool mountain weather, gardens, and scenic views from the cable cars. Ba Na Hills offers a calm escape from the city and a glimpse into both Vietnam’s history and modern creativity.

One of the modern symbols of Da Nang, the Dragon Bridge sits on the Han River, linking the Son Tra coastal area with the city center. Inspired by the Ly Dynasty Dragon, it represents strength and prosperity in Vietnamese culture.

On Friday, Saturday, and Sunday evenings at 9:00 PM, the bridge comes to life with a short fire and water show lasting about fifteen minutes. During this time, it’s closed to vehicles, but pedestrians gather to watch the lively performance.

After the show, visitors continue exploring nearby attractions such as the Son Tra Night Market, Love Bridge, and the Dragon Carp Statue. Other bridges along the Han River remain open, offering alternative views of the lightshow.
